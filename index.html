The 10 Key Models of Logistics

Logistics represents the physical movement and buffer storage of cargo throughout the global supply chain. Click on any model below to slide open detailed activities and examples.

01

Inbound Logistics

Movement of incoming parts & raw materials from suppliers to factory floor.

Key Activities: Purchasing, raw materials shipping, intake storage.
Example: Shipping container loads of fabric from Colombo to a garment manufacturer warehouse.
02

Outbound Logistics

Movement of completed commercial items from manufacturing to final users.

Key Activities: Finished goods storage, order processing, shipping container freight.
Example: Exporting finished rubber tires from Colombo port directly to automotive dealers in Hamburg.
03

Reverse Logistics

Handling returned goods, recycling, and recycling loop management.

Key Activities: Refurbishing, recycle sorting, return cargo logistics.
Example: Return of defective electronics to a service center in China for warranty replacement.
04

Third-Party (3PL)

Outsourcing primary warehousing and trucking services to an expert partner.

Key Activities: Shared warehouses, contracted truck fleets, customs brokerage.
Example: Nexus Cargo handles warehousing and customs processing for a consumer brands company.
05

Fourth-Party (4PL)

An integrator firm that oversees the entire supply chain network strategies.

Key Activities: Software integration, analytics, multi-vendor carrier management.
Example: A strategic partner managing three separate 3PLs and 5 ocean carriers for a retail giant.
06

Production Logistics

Managing components supply matching speed inside the production plant.

Key Activities: Just-In-Time (JIT) storage, automated guided vehicles, bin replenishments.
Example: Scheduling microchips supply lines to match automotive assembly speeds.
07

Distribution Logistics

Transport systems delivering products from regional distribution points.

Key Activities: Cross-docking operations, parcel dispatch hubs, grocery line distribution.
Example: Dispatching trucks to deliver daily fresh milk batches to national retail stores.
08

Military Logistics

Supply lines, armaments, and food logistics for defense forces.

Key Activities: Mobilization schedules, heavy airlift, tactical fuel support.
Example: Operating heavy military aircraft carriers carrying tactical trucks and food cargo.
09

Emergency Logistics

Rapid supply chain mobilization in response to crises and natural disasters.

Key Activities: Priority air cargo clearance, medical supply drops, emergency coordination.
Example: Shipping emergency vaccines and temporary tents to areas affected by a cyclone.
10

Green Logistics

Redesigning supply chains to minimize emissions and resource depletion.

Key Activities: Route optimization, biofuel shipping, recyclable plastic pallets.
Example: Switching from air shipping to eco-friendly rail/ocean freight to reduce carbon footprint.
